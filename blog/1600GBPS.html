CRS804 DDQ

Four 400 Gigabit ports. One rack unit. Enough switching bandwidth to move an entire SAN before you've finished saying "Have you tried turning it off and on again?"

Some Numbers To Frighten Accountants

There was a time when a 1 Gigabit switch was considered fast. Then came 10 Gigabit. Then 100 Gigabit. Now MikroTik has looked at all that and said: "No. More."

The CRS804 DDQ packs four QSFP56-DD ports capable of 400 Gigabit Ethernet each. That's a potential 1.6 Terabits of switching bandwidth sitting quietly in a compact 1U chassis. Not a chassis the size of a fridge. Not a chassis requiring its own postcode. A single rack unit.

"Network engineers will look at this and immediately begin inventing workloads for it."

What Is It?

The CRS804 DDQ is MikroTik's answer to the question nobody outside a datacentre would ever ask: "What if my server farm could inhale bandwidth?"

Designed for AI clusters, storage networks, aggregation layers, and modern high-performance fabrics, the CRS804 DDQ combines a Marvell switching ASIC, RouterOS v7, a quad-core 2GHz ARM CPU, 4GB of RAM, hot-swap cooling and redundant power supplies into a package that looks almost suspiciously affordable.

400G Ports

4 × QSFP56-DD ports capable of 400G operation. Because apparently 100 Gigabit is now considered quaint.

Flexible Breakouts

Breakouts to 200G, 100G, 50G and lower speeds allow it to talk to practically everything in your rack.

Redundancy

Dual hot-swap power supplies mean your switch survives when somebody inevitably kicks the wrong cable.

RouterOS

MikroTik's RouterOS provides a management environment that infrastructure people already know and secretly enjoy.

How Fast Is It?

Imagine driving a reasonably quick family hatchback. Now imagine replacing it with a spacecraft. That's roughly the scaling factor involved here.

A single port can deliver 400 Gigabits per second. Four ports can deliver 1.6 Terabits. The practical result is that storage arrays, GPU clusters and compute nodes stop complaining about the network and start blaming each other instead.

"At these speeds, the packets aren't travelling. They're teleporting."

This is precisely the sort of switch that finds itself in AI environments where GPUs cost more than houses, or storage fabrics where waiting two extra milliseconds could trigger a small riot.

The Verdict

★★★★★

The wonderful thing about the CRS804 DDQ is that it doesn't pretend to be anything else.

It isn't trying to power office phones. It isn't trying to run the entire enterprise from one box. It has one mission: move absurd amounts of data at wire speed.

For AI labs, storage fabrics, hyper-scale test environments, or anybody planning a journey into the world of 400 Gigabit Ethernet, this is a gloriously overpowered gateway drug.

Fast. Compact. Slightly ridiculous. Exactly what a Top Gear review likes to see.
