TOP GEAR FOR DATACENTRES

SUPERMICRO
AOC-S25GC-i2S

Two ports. Twenty-five gigabits per second each. Enough network bandwidth to make your storage array look nervous and your hypervisor grin like Clarkson discovering someone else is paying for the tyres.

What Is It?

Imagine a Ferrari engine. Now remove all the glamour, replace the noise with photons travelling through fibre optics and stick the whole thing into a 1U server. That's basically what we have here.

The Supermicro AOC-S25GC-i2S is a dual-port 25Gb Ethernet adapter built around Intel's E810-XXVAM2 controller. It plugs into a PCIe 4.0 x8 slot and exists for one purpose: moving alarming quantities of data whilst barely breaking a sweat.

"If a normal gigabit NIC is a family hatchback, this thing is a ballistic missile with an SNMP agent."

The Numbers

25 Gigabit Ethernet

Two SFP28 ports delivering enough throughput to thoroughly embarrass old 10GbE infrastructure.

PCIe 4.0

Modern, fast and backwards compatible. Like a time traveller who still understands VHS.

RDMA

Supports RoCEv2 and iWARP, reducing latency to levels where packets hardly have time to become emotionally attached to the wire.

Virtualisation Ready

SR-IOV, DPDK, VXLAN, NVGRE and Geneve support mean it feels entirely at home among large fleets of virtual machines and containers.

On The Road*

*Road in this case meaning rack row.

Install this card in a database server, storage node, Kubernetes worker or virtualisation host and something remarkable happens:

The network stops being the bottleneck.

Suddenly the CPUs are busy. The SSDs are busy. The storage fabric is busy. Everything else is sweating. The network card, meanwhile, is casually leaning against a rack rail asking whether anyone has anything harder.

Intel's Application Device Queues (ADQ) and Dynamic Device Personalisation (DDP) are aimed at squeezing latency out of demanding applications, while hardware offloads ensure the CPU spends more time doing useful work and less time acting as a postal worker for packets.

The Verdict

Rating: ★★★★★

The AOC-S25GC-i2S isn't exciting in the way a V12 supercar is exciting.

It's exciting in the way discovering your backup window has shrunk from eight hours to forty minutes is exciting.

It is unapologetically aimed at serious infrastructure: hypervisors, cloud platforms, storage fabrics, AI clusters and data-centre operators who consider "a bit of bandwidth" to mean something measured in tens of gigabits.

Fast. Capable. Slightly ridiculous. Just how we like it.
